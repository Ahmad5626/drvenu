What is General Orthopedics?

General orthopedics focuses on the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of disorders related to bones, joints, muscles, ligaments, and tendons. This field addresses both acute injuries and long-term musculoskeletal conditions for patients of all ages.

Common Conditions Treated:
  • Fractures: From simple to complex breaks in any part of the body.
  • Arthritis: Management of osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and other degenerative joint conditions.
  • Sprains and Strains: Muscle or ligament injuries caused by accidents or overuse.
  • Back and Neck Pain: Addressing spinal conditions and postural issues.
  • Joint Pain: Knee, hip, shoulder, or other joint discomfort.
  • Pediatric Orthopedic Issues: Congenital conditions or injuries in children.
Treatment Options Available:
  • Non-Surgical Management: Medications, braces, physiotherapy, and lifestyle guidance.
  • Surgical Solutions: When necessary, procedures like joint repair or replacement.
  • Preventive Care: Counseling on posture, exercise, and injury prevention.
